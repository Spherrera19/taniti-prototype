Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know for your Taniti visit

What is the drinking age on Taniti? +

The drinking age on Taniti is 18 years old, and it is not strictly enforced. However, alcohol cannot be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 AM.

What currency is accepted? +

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its primary currency, but many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and most businesses accept major credit cards.

What languages are spoken? +

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, making communication easy for tourists. However, very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by older residents.

Are there medical facilities? +

Yes, there is one hospital and several clinics on the island. The hospital has many multilingual employees to assist international visitors.

How safe is Taniti for tourists? +

Taniti is very safe for visitors. Violent crime is extremely rare on the island. As tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes, so visitors should take normal precautions with valuables.

What about power outlets and electricity? +

Power outlets are 120 volts, the same as in the United States, so American travelers won't need voltage converters.

Are there national holidays that affect tourism? +

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on these holidays. Visitors should plan their activities accordingly and check ahead.
